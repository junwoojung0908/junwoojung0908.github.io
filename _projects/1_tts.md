Studying how time-to-solution scales with approximation ratio _r_ in hybrid neutral-atom optimization. Comparing annealing vs. quenching strategies on King's-lattice MIS instances using QuEra's Aquila platform.

A shell model predicts a sharp crossover in TTS(_r_) at a critical approximation ratio _r_\*: annealing is exponentially favored for near-exact targets, while quenching is favored for relaxed targets.

**Keywords:** QuEra Aquila · TTS(r) · Annealing vs. quenching · MIS
